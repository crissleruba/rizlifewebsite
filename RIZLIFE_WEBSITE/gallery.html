Important Scenes

Simoun on the Steamship Tabo

The novel opens aboard the steamship Tabo, traveling along the Pasig River. Simoun, now a wealthy jeweler, is seen observing the passengers, including Basilio, Isagani, and Ben Zayb. This scene sets the tone for the novel, introducing Simoun’s revenge-driven transformation.

Basilio and Simoun in the Cemetery

Basilio visits the grave of his mother, Sisa, and encounters Simoun. Simoun, who is really Crisostomo Ibarra in disguise, tries to convince Basilio to join his revolutionary cause, revealing his plan to overthrow the Spanish government.

The Wedding Lamp Scene

Simoun executes his grand plan for revenge by planting a time bomb disguised as a beautiful lamp at the wedding of Paulita Gomez and Juanito Pelaez. His goal is to kill the Spanish elite and corrupt officials attending the event. However, Isagani learns about it and, driven by his love for Paulita, throws the lamp into the river, preventing the explosion.

Death and Confession

After his plans fail, Simoun is fatally wounded and seeks refuge with Padre Florentino. Before dying, he confesses his true identity as Ibarra and admits that his revenge has only led to more suffering. Padre Florentino throws Simoun’s remaining treasures into the sea, symbolizing the futility of wealth and vengeance.
